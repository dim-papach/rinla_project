Fix your FITS (fyf)

Resolving satellite, cosmic rays and bad pixels in astronomical images

Abstract

Meteors, satellites, cosmic rays, and bad pixels are just a few of the obstacles that create artifacts in images and complicate the interpretation of astronomical observations. Satellites are a very common problem nowadays due to the launch of thousands of them by various companies and nations around the world. With limited time and resources, every astronomical image holds significant value, and often astronomers must work with compromised images. By employing the Integrated Nested Laplace Approximation (INLA) algorithm, which offers a quicker alternative to the Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method, we have created an open-source command-line interface (CLI) tool that can restore images. The algorithm has been applied to the planetary nebulae NGC 7009 and the HII region M16 ¬†with an accuracy between 70-99% (Structural similarity index measure; SSIM) and a residual percentage between -8% and 8% with a mean residual close to 0.

Aims

We created the FYF command-line interface (CLI) tool to process astronomical FITS images. The tool is to use the R-INLA package to handle various artifacts such as:

  • cosmic rays,
  • satellite trails,
  • bad pixels,
  • other astronomical objects, such as Low Ionization Structures (LIS)

INLA

Integrated Nested Laplace Approximation (INLA) algorithm is a potentially quicker alternative to the Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method, which is commonly used for Bayesian inference for latent Gaussian models. INLA is particularly useful for spatial data analysis, making it suitable for processing astronomical images. By applying INLA, the algorithm treats the corrupted image as a latent field, which we want to recover. The output of the fyf algorithm is a filled image where the missing or corrupted pixels are estimated based on the surrounding pixel values.

FYF Testing

  • We use two astronomical FITS images:
    • Planetary nebulae NGC 7009
    • HII region M16
  • Introduce artifacts in images (cosmic rays and satellite trails), and process them.
  • Compare the processed and the original using:
    • Residual percentage and standard deviation
    • Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM)

FYF Command-Line Usage

$  fyf process --cosmic_fraction 20% --number_of_satellites 5 --input input_file.fits --output output_file.fits
